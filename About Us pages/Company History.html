    • Company History


    1979

    When McDonalds first arrived in Singapore, they opened their first outlet at the Liat Towers located on Orchard Road, setting a record for the most hamburgers served in a day.


    1980

    McDonalds launches their iconic brithday parties.


    1982

    East Coast Park welcomes McDonalds first Drive-Thru, granting access to another way to get food from McDonalds. This same outlet would later get Singapore's first Skate-Thru in 2003.


    1989

    The Ronald McDonald House Charities (RMHC) is incepted into Singapore, with the singular mission of directly improving the health and well-being of Singaporean children.


    1991

    McDonalds introduces playgrounds to its premises, becoming one of the first restaurants to introduce a playground on its premises, allowing families to have fun and create memories together.


    1992

    McDonalds introduces nutritional information on their menus in support of the Singapore’s National Healthy Lifestyle campaign. McDonalds offered an Eat Light Menu offering under-500 calorie meals.


    1995

    The McSpicy is created and debuts in Singapore, having become a Singaporean favourite ever since its creation.


    1999

    The McSpicy debuts in Singapore, instantly becoming successful and a Singaporean favourite.


    2003

    The First McCafé in Singapore opens up at McDonald's Great World City. The first McDonald's Skate-Thru is introduced at the East Coast Park McDonalds outlet.


    2005

    Prosperity Burger debuts in Singapore and has since become a must-have with McDonald's Twister Fries every Lunar New Year. 24/7 McDelivery Service is introduced in Singapore, allowing Singaporeans to get their meals at any time. Following the introduction of the 24/7 McDelivery Service, selected restaraunts begin operating around the clock. McDonalds also introduces their Mix N' Match Happy Meals, which allows parents to create a balanced meal by choosing an entrée, a side and drink from a wide range of choices ranging from Low-Fat Milk and 100% Apple Juice to Corn Cup and Apple Slices.


    2008

    For the first time ever, McDonalds offers children the chance-of-a-lifetime experience, which is the opportunity to join more than 300 children from all over the world in Beijing for the Beijing 2008 Olympics as part of the McDonald’s Champion Kids programme.


    2010

    McDonalds celebrates their 30 year anniversary of being with Singapore, which is a milestone in their Singaporean journey.


    2011

    McDonald's green flagship restaurant at Jurong Central Park becomes the first restaurant in Singapore to be awarded the BCA Green Mark Platinum Award, the highest accolade in the newly created Green Mark for restaurants.


    2012

    One of McDonald's restaurant managers wins the prestigious SuperStar Award at the Excellent Service Award (EXSA) for the fourth time, a testament to the strong service culture upheld at McDonald’s.


    2013

    On 18 March 2013, to mark National Breakfast Day in Singapore, 100,000 Egg McMuffins are given out free to spread the good word about breakfast.


    2017

    McDonalds introduces the role of Guest Experience and Family ambassadors in their restaurants to engage with families and offer table service and their warm touch of hospitality.


    2018

    McDonalds offers the choice of making donations to Ronald McDonald House Charities via the self-ordering kiosks in their restaurants.


    2020

    McDonalds brand new family flagship restaurant opens at Canberra Plaza with the most exciting PlayPlace for kids.


    2021

    The McSpicy, which was introduced over 20 years ago in 1998, returns to the McDonalds menu, delighting customers with its crispy, juicy, tender flavour.
